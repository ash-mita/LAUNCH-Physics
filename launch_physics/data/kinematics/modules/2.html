The amount of distance that Alice travelled is the length of the blue path, while her displacement is represented by the red arrow. Displacement is the net change in Alice’s position. Displacement is vector, because it depends on the position of an object, and the direction matters.

Velocity is the change in position of an object over time. The speed is the magnitude of the velocity, or the size of the velocity vector. Usually the speed is associated with the value of the velocity. The equation for velocity is given by v = meters/seconds, and the unit for velocity is m/s.

Acceleration is the change in velocity over time of an object. Acceleration is essentially the amount that the velocity changes every second. Think about a car as it starts to drive. Its velocity increases, which means that the car is accelerating.

The amount of distance that an object has travelled does not have anything to do with direction.

  • Displacement - Position of an object
  • Velocity - The change in position over time
  • Acceleration - The derivative of velocity with respect to time
