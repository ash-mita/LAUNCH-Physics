In physics, we describe motion using vectors and scalars. Vectors have a magnitude and direction, while scalars only have a magnitude. The magnitude refers to the size of the vector or scalar. Some examples of vectors are displacement, acceleration, velocity, and forces, and some examples of scalars are mass, distance, and speed. We will cover the definitions of these terms later on.
